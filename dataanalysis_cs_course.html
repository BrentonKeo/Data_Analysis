Question: What are the differences in tiers?

                What are tiers?

                Tiers are a way of determining how successful the playable characters are in comparison to other playable characters, ranging from best to worst over 6 different tiers. These are listed under the ‘name’ column.

  • God
  • S
  • A
  • B
  • C
  • D

God tier which according to the data, is comprised of those which have the highest scores. When determining a playable character’s score, each of the data points of the playable character’s set is factored in. Within the given categories, the win percentage and the KDA (Kill/Death/Assist) are seemingly the biggest contributing factors into the classification of the tiers.

                Between God tier and S tier, surprisingly, the win rates were identical. It was the KDA that seemed to have the biggest influence on the separation of the top two tiers.

                Lastly, I compared the data of tier S and tier D. The difference of win percentage between tier S and tier D is approximately 0.02321 with the difference of KDA between tier S and tier D is 0.28977.

                In conclusion, the presumed difference of win percentages wasn’t as significant from tier to tier. KDA was more influential than the win percentages. Surprisingly enough, win percentages ended up having the little variance across all tiers.
